# SecondPlay

> An invite-only toy exchange app for a Dutch school community, where children list
> their outgrown toys, earn real money, and learn that the things they own have value.

## The problem
Every week, a perfectly good toy gets thrown away at a school — not because it broke,
but because no child knew another child wanted it. At the same time, a family three
classrooms away buys the exact same toy, new, still in plastic wrap.

SecondPlay closes that loop.

## What it does
- Children (age 6–12) lead the listing flow: photos, price, description
- Parents approve listings before they go live — the app is not a bypass; it is a bridge
- Families browse and send Deal Requests within their trusted school community
- Exchanges happen at the school gate — no shipping, no strangers
- Sellers see their Earnings Tracker update in real time

## Why it matters
This is not a marketplace with an educational feature. It is an educational product
with a marketplace mechanic. The sale *is* the lesson.

## My role
Solo founder and builder — product discovery, UX design, technical architecture,
and implementation.

## Approach
Invite-only v1 at one school in the Netherlands. No app stores. No public sign-up.
WhatsApp-native distribution. Privacy-first: no school-identifiable data stored.
